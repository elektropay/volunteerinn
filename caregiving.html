Project Description

Life remai ns a challenge for people with disabilities and the elderly in Mexico. Abuse and neglect are commonly found in publicly-funded institutions. Private institutions and NGOs provide care and support to make up for the lack of institutional oversight and funding to serve these populations. Volunteers aid these organizations by bringing energy, love and dedication to help local staff in local caregiving projects.

Volunteers in caregiving projects work in shelters and clinics for the elderly and physically and mentally disabled. They make up for lack of staff to give individual support to the residents and patients of these facilities. Volunteers assist local staff with daily activities to activate the residents and patients mentally and physically. They are welcome to bring their own workshops and creative activities to the projects. Additionally, volunteers assist with feeding the patients and helping them with their physical therapy sessions, and most importantly, they give the residents and patients the love and attention that they need.

Special Needs Care - Mérida

Volunteers are welcomed with open arms on our Special Needs project in Mérida. Volunteers work alongside local staff in a structured learning center and residency for adolescents and adults with physical and/or mental disabilities that have been abandoned by their families. The work with this project includes helping teachers with daily operations and classroom maintenance, going on outings with residents, and overseeing simple activities with small groups of students.

Volunteers should come with open hearts, patience and creativity. Their role is to assist with a variety of projects from education and mental development to physical therapy and creative arts. Volunteers give the residents at the shelter the specialized attention that they need and often lack because of limited staff and personnel. They are invited to bring their own workshops for the residents if they are interested in music, dance, and art.

Volunteer tasks include helping with daily operations, preparing food, serving meals and cleaning. Many residents require physical therapy that is given on-site and take classes in basic skills. This home is run by a religious order and volunteer work can involve assisting with fundraising efforts to benefit the residents. These residential sites are funded solely by donations.
